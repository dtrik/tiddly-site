Hyperfocus

The Limits of Your Attention

There is a finite points to how many things we can focus on. We typically get around 11M bits of information as sensory experience each second according to Timothy Wilson from University of Virginia. We can consciously process and focus on only 40 bits from that at once.

Even processing a conversation consumes more than half of our attention according to Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi.

Our short term memory can hold even lesser information. This memory enables us to think about what we are doing as we do it. Typically it is four chunks of information and at most seven.

Attentional space is the amount of mental capacity we have available to focus on and process things in the moment. Our focus and attentional space are responsible for most of our conscious experiences. It has to be managed well as it is small and can hold only a few things.
